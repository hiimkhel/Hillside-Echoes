GAMEON:HOWBLOCKBLAST IMPROVES TEEN COGNITIVE SKILLS

BY SHEKINAH ORDUYO

Many individuals believe video games are merely a method for teenagers to squander their time, but certain games can genuinely enhance cognitive function.

A prime example is BlockBlast, a mobile puzzle game that pushes players to think strategically. For adolescents, whose brains are still maturing, games like BlockBlast can aid in cognitive development. BlockBlast can assist in boosting focus, memory, and decision-making among teens.

During adolescence, the brain continues to evolve. Improving cognitive skills is crucial for enhancing problem-solving abilities, concentration, and planning. When teenagers participate in activities that stimulate these skills, they can reinforce their cognitive capabilities.

Puzzle games, which necessitate strategy and critical thought, can serve as effective tools for this mental exercise. BlockBlast is a game in which players must arrange various shaped blocks into a grid, clearing complete rows or columns to accumulate points. As the game progresses and becomes more challenging, players are required to think ahead and carefully plan their moves. This transforms BlockBlast into more than just an entertaining game; it evolves into a cognitive workout. Engaging in the game can enhance spatial reasoning, working memory, and concentration—traits essential for achieving success in academics and daily life.

In addition to its cognitive advantages, BlockBlast teaches players to make wiser decisions. With no option to undo previous moves, every action is significant.

Teens learn to slow down, contemplate before taking action, and handle the results of their decisions. These competencies are valuable not only within the game but also in real-life scenarios such as studying, managing time, and resolving issues under stress. Developing such abilities can relate directly to everyday situations.

Indeed, BlockBlast is a game that can support cognitive development in teenagers. While it’s essential to manage screen time and maintain balance, playing games like BlockBlast in moderation can actually help adolescents enhance their thinking skills. Instead of perceiving video games as unproductive, we should acknowledge their potential to facilitate learning in enjoyable and engaging ways.

BEHIND THE MIND: MENTAL HEALTH AWARENESS

BY SHEKINAH ORDUYO

Living in a world that moves faster everyday, it's easy to overlook what's happening beneath the surface and mental health often tells a different story.

In school, we learn about the heart, the lungs, different organs, and how our bodies work, but what about the brain and our emotions. Mental health affects how we think, feel, and act, yet it's often left out of the conversation. While physical health often receives significant attention, mental health struggles frequently, mainly hidden, stigmatized, and undertreated. For decades, mental health was misunderstood—often seen as weakness, or worse, ignored altogether.

Mental health encompasses our emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It influences how we think, feel, and act, affecting our ability to manage stress, build relationships, and make healthy choices. Conditions like depression, anxiety, schizophrenia, and bipolar disorder are not simply weaknesses; they are complex illnesses with biological, psychological, and social dimensions.

According to the World Health Organization, over 970 million people worldwide live with a mental health disorder. That’s nearly 1 in 8 people. The consequences of untreated mental illness extend far beyond the individual. Untreated mental health conditions can strain family relationships,

Untreated mental health conditions can strain family relationships, impact productivity in the workplace or in school, and place a significant burden on healthcare systems worldwide.

Mental health encompasses more than just biological factors. Social contexts, trauma, genetics, and even dietary and sleep patterns contribute to it. This complexity also indicates that there are numerous methods to enhance mental health.

Identifying the signs and symptoms of mental illness, such as ongoing sadness, anxiety, alterations in sleep habits, or withdrawal from social interactions, is a crucial first step. It is essential to seek assistance from a mental health professional, like a psychiatrist, psychologist, or counselor. Various effective treatments, including therapy, medication, and lifestyle changes, are available to help alleviate symptoms and enhance overall wellness.

Creating a world where mental health is prioritized requires a collective effort. Education is vital for dispelling misconceptions and promoting awareness. Open conversations about mental health within families, schools, workplaces, and communities are essential to break down the stigma.

By simply working together, we can create a world where mental well-being is valued, understood, and supported, ensuring that everyone has the opportunity to thrive.

EDUCATION'S QUIET CRISIS: LITERACY AND NUMERACY PROBLEMS AT SCHOOL

By: Pauline Vilavicencio

Education has been considered the key to a better future for long, especially in our country, the Philippines. Where young people carry the hopes of their families and communities. For Filipino youth, education is more than just an experience and requirement— it is a stepping stone to opportunities, self-confidence, and meaningful citizenship. However, behind those high rates of graduates is a quiet crisis that covers the image of every student on the stage.

This isn’t just a fundamental issue, this does not circulate in one neighborhood or one specific school. It runs in every vein of the country, and it hides in a plain sight.

In 2021, the Philippines had a high literacy rate of 99.27 percent, which is a positive achievement. However, in 2022, the World Bank classified 90 percent of Filipino children aged 10 as learning poor, indicating their inability to read and understand a simple text by that age and most of them suffers in poverty which affects their opportunity to enter higher education.

In fact, this crisis happens for a reason that students lack on motivation, prior knowledge, and access to learning resources. People struggles with literacy because of learning disabilities, having limited English and Filipino language skills, limited access to books, and a school’s ability to meet each student’s reading challenges. Some reasons is because the school’s curriculum in most schools is overcrowded, and basic literacy and numeracy may receive less focus as students progress past the primary years. Large classes, limited resources, and demands are the challenges that teachers have to deal with.

I stand for the fact that literacy and numeracy should be treated as essential, lifelong skills and not boxes to be ticked in elementary school. Schools should employ teachers with great literacy and numeracy teaching methods and refocus the curriculum to prioritize literacy and numeracy mastery and lastly, lower the number of students per section to educate easily.

Literacy and numeracy aren’t options for acquiring skills. They are the key for every other kind of learning and for participation in adult life. If schools can’t deliver them, everything else falls apart. It’s time to stop treating them like boxes to be ticked and start treating them like the priority they are.
