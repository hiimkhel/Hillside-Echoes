Iloilo Provincial Government launches teen center version 2.0

BY PRECIOUS COLEEN CUBIN

The Iloilo Provincial Government held a groundbreaking ceremony of the two-storey teen center version 2.0 building at Alimodian National Comprehensive High School.

The teen center is the flagship project of the Iloilo Provincial Government under the mantra, MoRProGRes Iloilo or A Movement for a Robust, Progressive, Globally Competitive and Resilient Province of Iloilo.

“It will be a haven where they can develop their personality, talents, skills, and a sanctuary to enhance their well-being, and prepare themselves for the challenges and triumphs of the future,” Atty. Ruel Von Superio, Provincial Population Officer, said during the groundbreaking ceremony.

Governor Athur Defensor Jr. also added that building teen centers at schools provides an avenue for youths to learn values and knowledge which can’t be learned inside the classroom.

Moreover, Defensor allocated funds to renovate the classrooms of the school under the BES Probins or the Bulig Eskwela sang Probinsya program.

The groundbreaking ceremony was attended by Governor Arthur Defensor Jr., Mayor Ian Kenneth Alfeche of Alimodian, Mayor Lee Ann Debuque, who represented the League of Municipalities of the Philippines, Provincial Population Officer Atty. Ruel Von Superio, Provincial Engineer Romeo Andig, ANCHS Principal Romar C. Cubin, and other provincial and local officials.

ALIMODIAN HOSTS CDSA II

BY NICOLE A. ALIPIN AND ASHLEY NICOLE T. TOLENTINO

ALIMODIAN, ILOILO—With the theme “Atletang Palaban, Nangingibabaw sa Diwa ng Makabansang Paligsahan,” hundreds of student-athletes across the second district of Iloilo gathered in the municipality of Alimodian for the CDSA II Meet 2025.

The athletic meet is a five-day event that brought together young athletes to compete, grow, and build camaraderie.

Sports is a platform for young athletes to continue their studies as it provides opportunities like scholarships in college that’s why the government will help through various projects to show support to our athletes, Cong. Michael B. Gorriceta.

The hosting of the municipality during the CDSA II meet served as an avenue to improve and identify the lacking facilities of the LGU and the school in sports.

Moreover, Cong. Jojo Ang of Uswag Ilonggo Partylist promised several projects in the school through the construction of an oval for athletics and several classrooms in Alimodian National Comprehensive High School to cater the number of learners.

On the other hand, Dr. Kathy Gorriceta reminded the athletes, coaches, and supporters to maintain honesty, to dream bigger despite the challenges encountered, and always stay grounded.

The CDSA II meet is composed of eight municipalities in the province of Iloilo, namely Alimodian, Leganes, Leon, New Lucena, Pavia, San Miguel, Santa Barbara, and Zarraga.

The different sport events officially began on January 16 with different venues across the municipality of Alimodian. The competition ended on January 18 with a Closing Ceremony and Awarding Program at ANCHS Grounds and outstanding athletes and teams were recognized for their efforts.

Meanwhile, CDSA II Meet is an avenue for Western Visayas to look for delegates for the Palarong Pambansa.

The Hubon competition is a yearly activity that is organized by the local government unit of Alimodian.

Figueroa, Quijano recieve Supreme Debates Year 4 awards

BY PRECIOUS COLEEN CUBIN

English club president, Roniel John Figueroa and The Hillside Echoes editorial writer, Josephine Marie Quijano garnered awards during The Supreme Debates Year 4 held at Iloilo Provincial Capitol.

Figueroa garnered the best speaker and the best debater awards, while Quijano received the best rebuttalist award.

“Well, I am always grateful to receive these awards. I was first in doubt if I could achieve these awards because the proposition was more in favor of the opposing side, especially for my role as the practicability,” Figueroa said.

The team elaborated that preparation is the key in succeeding in their previous competitions.

“When I entered debate, the first thing they said is that the most prepared team wins. Ever since then I stood by that phrase every time we prepared for our next match,” Figueroa added.

In addition, he noted the significant role played by his advisers, his team, and his seniors in achieving these milestones

“I still owe it to my team, Sir Fermo, Ma'am Dolly and Manang Hannah that I was able to reach these milestones. Yet I believe there is more room for improvement during my time at the podium,” said Figueroa.

For two consecutive years, the Alimodian Debate Team has remained on the top in the debate tilt.

The Alimodian Debate Team is composed of participants from Alimodian National Comprehensive High school, Sinamay National High School, and Dalid National High School.

ANCHS TEACHERS SHINE IN INNOVATION RESEARCH SHOWDOWN

BY ASHLEY NICOLE T. TOLENTINO

Teachers from Alimodian National Comprehensive High School (ANCHS) brought pride to the institution after earning distinctions in the Schools Division of Iloilo (SDO Iloilo) 4th Division Innovation and Research Showdown held from November 13 to 15, 2024, held in Iloilo City, and the Division Research Congress on December 13, 2024, in Guimaras Province.

Mrs. Angie G. Alonsabe, Head Teacher in Mathematics, earned 1st Runner-Up in the Innovation Category for her project titled PINDOTA—a novel interactive approach esigned to boost students’ mathematical proficiency through engaging student-centered strategies.

Dr. Kim Jann Calibara, Dr. April Mae Lumabit, and Mrs. Marjorie Penar, Senior High School mathematics teachers,

were named Division Finalists in both the Best Research Paper and Best Research Poster categories.

In addition, Dr. Calibara was named a Finalist for Best in Presentation, underscoring his excellence in delivering research-based educational initiatives.

Meanwhile, Calibara, Lumabit, and Penar, received the Research Efficiency Award in the Division Research Congress, recognizing their timely, relevant, and high-quality research outputs that contribute significantly to teaching and learning improvements.

The school’s continued pursuit in research and innovation reaffirms its commitment to a culture of inquiry, collaboration, and continuous improvement that inspires both students and educators.

ANCHS JOINS REGIONAL ENGLISH CHALLENGE

BY ASHLEY NICOLE T. TOLENTINO

The English Department of Alimodian National Comprehensive High School (ANCHS) achieved a remarkable feat after placing Top 10 out of 30 participating schools in the first-ever Regional English Challenge, held on November 21, 2024, at West Visayas State University.

Marcia Ysabelle Ampatin (grade 8) got 7th place in the Spelling Quiz while the team of Edwin Levin Alonsabe (grade 9), Hannah Mendote (grade 8), and Colleen Almarines (grade 9) bagged 9th place in the team event in Literature Quiz.

The regional contest brought together top-performing students from schools throughout the region, who competed in the areas of Grammar, Literature, and Spelling. This activity was sponsored by the West Visayas State University College of Education.

The ANCHS contestants who also included Ashley Nicole Tolentino, Grace Alingalan, and Anne Pauleen Tabangcurda were coached by Mrs. Jasmine Amparo, Mrs. Jessica Desoy, Ms. Zennia Anila, and Mrs. Jaisa Mhe Lerasan.
